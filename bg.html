Problem Identification

Being a non-stock, non-profit organization, Talikala, Inc. is currently dealing with a major problem that affects the majority of its operations as an organization, namely their financial difficulty in providing them with the resources, facilities, and support they need to help the affected and their families. Talikala and the executives have been dealing with this issue for as long as any other organization because they are not paid for their work and receive no value in return; as a result, they depend on benefactors and donors to help them obtain services that can sustain the entire community on a daily basis. However, despite the fact that they have donors, it is vital to confront the reality that it is insufficient to meet everyone's requirements either. Hence, as their vision and mission statements indicate, they envision a gender-fair relationship between women and men, who enjoy equality and full participation in the development of people and society through the development of prostituted women and girls' abilities, among other things, the community is hindered in advancing its advocacy due to a lack of funding to acquire necessary material items and objectives for their institution and for their beneficiaries.

One With Talikala

Project Background


Our project, One with Talikala, is a fundraising and donation campaign which will be done in a timeframe of 2 months and will be primarily operated in our group’s respective households and the AdDU community. The fundraising campaign of the project aims to raise a total of P10,000-P15,000 through our service intervention that we will also use to teach the beneficiaries of Talikala as a way of their livelihood. Additionally, the donation campaign encourages people from the AdDU community to contribute their unused resources through our “donation box” at Gate 12 which we will collect and sell online to gather more profit to be given to Talikala. All profit from both campaigns of the project will be used to buy school materials for the children in the institution. These two campaigns for our project will be disseminated and operated through social media exposure and the use of publication materials in order to reach our objective of helping our partner institution by providing them with their sufficient needs.

Project Significance

Since Talikala Inc. is a non-stock, non-profit, and non-governmental organization, our project aims to be the bridge between good-hearted individuals who are willing to help Talikala, Inc. In the context of our project, it signifies the needs of the institution primarily in the education of the children of Talikala which is the objective that our project aims to address in easing the financial problem and the lack of resources of the institution. Through this, it can pave the way for continuing the advocacy of Talikala and its programs internally and externally for other prostituted women and children in Davao City and for the awareness of the public.
